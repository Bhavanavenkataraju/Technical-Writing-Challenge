What is the Internet?

Have you ever wondered what the Internet exactly is and how it works?

The Internet is a vast network that connects millions of computers worldwide. Through the internet, people can share information and communicate with almost anybody in the world.

In other words, it creates a communication medium to share and get information online. If your device is connected to the internet then you will be able to access all the applications, websites, social media apps, and many more services. It is one of the fastest mediums for sending and receiving information.

Imagine you want to share a picture with your friend who is in Norway! If both of your devices are connected to the internet, then you can share the image with your friend who is miles away from you in just less than a second.

To keep it simple, the actual working of the internet takes place with the help of physical cables, which can include copper telephone wires, TV cables, and fiber optic cables. Even wireless connections like Wi-Fi and 3G/4G rely on these physical cables to access the internet.

It works on the concept of clients and servers. Here, the client is your device that is connected to the internet, and the servers are the computers where all the websites are stored.

When you visit a website, your device sends a request over the physical cables to the server. As soon as the request arrives, the server retrieves the website and sends the data back to your computer.
