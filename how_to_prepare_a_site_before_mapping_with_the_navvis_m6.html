How to prepare a site before mapping with the NavVis M6?

This instruction explains how to prepare a site before mapping, and maintain the agreed mapping schedule, thus generating high-quality data.

Pre-mapping checklist to ensure a fluent mapping process

The following checklist helps to ensure a fluent mapping process:

  1. Plan Ahead

    Plan your mapping route in advance.

  2. Overview

    Get an overview of your building to be prepared for the occurring cases.

  3. Room Division

    Divide your mapping area into zones by dividing your object into sections containing one dataset each. Map as much as possible within a single dataset floor to reduce the time during post-processing and dataset alignment stages.

    Note:

    One mapping session should not exceed 45 minutes.

  4. Avoid Pedestrians and Spectators

    Ensure the entrances to the mapping area are blocked (i.e. with barrier tape), so no one appears in the images unexpectedly. In case blocking the area is impossible, we suggest you exhibit some of our NavVis info leaflets that help quickly explain to curious pedestrians and spectators why they should stay out of the mapping area.

  5. Clean up

    Ensure all rooms and objects are cleaned appropriately to obtain desirable images. To protect any confidential information, activate a screen saver and remove any confidential files and objects before mapping.

  6. Sufficient lighting

    Ensure proper lighting to obtain better quality images. Turn on the lights and allow natural lighting by opening the curtains or by deactivating the solar shading. Furthermore, turn-off motion-activated lights and provide additional lighting in dark areas. Overexposure to sunlight may also depreciate the image quality. For example, during sunrise or sunset.

You are now ready to map your space with the NavVis M6 Mobile Mapping System. Happy Mapping!
